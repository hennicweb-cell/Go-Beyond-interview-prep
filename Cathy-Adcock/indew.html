STAR Method — How to Structure Your Answers

The STAR framework — your guide for every competency question

SSituationSet the scene briefly. What was the context? What charity, what programme, what was the starting point?
TTaskWhat did you need to do? What was the challenge, the objective, the problem to solve?
AActionWhat did YOU specifically do? Not "we" — "I". Be concrete and specific about your personal contribution.
RResultWhat happened? What was the outcome? Give a number wherever you can. What did you learn?

Aim for 3–4 minutes per STAR answer. Practice out loud before the day — the difference between a prepared and an unprepared STAR answer is enormous. Don't read from notes; tell the story.

📢 "Tell me about a campaign you owned end-to-end where something went wrong" Suggested: Christmas Appeal 2022
S
Julian House, December 2022. We had secured pro bono animation from a creative agency for the first time — an exciting opportunity to run an integrated Christmas appeal with shared creative across direct mail and digital.
T
The agency delivered the final campaign creative on 1 December — the start of our Christmas appeal window. The digital and direct mail elements were no longer aligned with each other and could not both use the same creative to deadline.
A
I made the decision to decouple the two campaigns and run them with different approaches. I kept the integrated elements where I could, but gave both campaigns distinct messaging and creative to work within the constraints. I managed the whole pivot myself under maximum time pressure.
R
We delivered the most successful Christmas appeal Julian House had ever run — just over £200,000, an exceptional ROI because the pro bono creative had cost us nothing. The crisis became an opportunity because we stayed calm, adapted fast, and executed.

📢 "How would you grow regular giving from £90k with no existing stewardship?" Suggested approach — not STAR, but structured
First
Understand the data. Who are the 200–300 regular givers? How did they come to us? What else do they do with the charity — are they also volunteers, challenge event participants, or legacy pledgers? That segmentation tells me everything about which stewardship approaches will resonate.
Then
Build a basic stewardship journey — a thank-you communication with impact stories, quarterly updates, an anniversary touchpoint with cumulative giving total. This costs very little and immediately stops the current silence. Retention is cheaper than acquisition.
Acquisition
Start with warm conversion from one-off donors who have given multiple times — they're already engaged, lower cost to convert. Build the business case for Sara from the known lifetime value of existing regular givers — what does a £10/month giver return over three years? That's the ROI argument for the spend.
Measure
Track retention rate month by month. Track conversion rate from warm donors. Report to Sara against those KPIs. If retention improves by even 10%, the income impact is significant without spending anything on acquisition.
